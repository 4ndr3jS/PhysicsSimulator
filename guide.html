Basic Controls

Each simulation has interactive control that let you manipulate parametars and observe how they affect the system. Here are the common controls you'll find:

Sliders: Adjust physical parametars like mass, velocity, force, or angle in real-time
Play / Pause: Start or stop the simulation to observe diffrent properties
Reset: Return to its inital state
Click & Drag: Many simulations let you interact directly with objects

How to Learn Effectively

1. Study with Observation

Run the simulation with the deualt settings first. Watch what happens and try to predict the outcome before changing anything.

2. Change One Thing at a Time

To understand cause and effect of a certain variable, don't change all things at once.

3. Text Extreme Values

Text extreme values to see how the system behaves. Who know maybe you'll discover something new.

3. Connect to Theory

Ofcourse, after experimenting, you need to connect the information you have just seen with theory. To do that visit the Theory page!

Common Scenarios to Explore

Projectile Motion

What happens when you throw an object at a 45 degree angle vs other angles. Which angle gives the maximum height? Maximum distance?

Pendulum

Adjust the length and initial angle. Does the period change when adjusting the mass?

Collisions

Compare elastic vs inelastic collisions. What is the diffrance between them. Is anything conserved?

Wave Interference

Create constructive and deconstructive interference patterns. Is photon a wave or a particle?
